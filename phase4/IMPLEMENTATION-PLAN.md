# Phase 4 Implementation Plan — Vertical Intelligence and Booking

## Mission

Transform Ringback from a generic three-question callback workflow into a controlled, versioned vertical-intelligence platform that can qualify callers appropriately and create real bookings without making unsafe promises or double-booking limited capacity.

Phase 4 answers:

> Can Ringback adapt safely to different service verticals and convert qualified demand into a confirmed next action while preserving deterministic controls, human escalation, and tenant isolation?

## Position analysis

The tempting move is to put a large language model in the call and tell it to behave like a receptionist. That creates impressive demos but exposes the business to hidden tactical losses:

1. **Prompt drift** — a model can change behavior without an explicit product release.
2. **Vertical confusion** — a plumbing workflow can ask irrelevant questions for dental, automotive, or appliance repair.
3. **Unsafe advice** — emergency, medical, legal, electrical, gas, or pricing language may exceed the approved scope.
4. **False availability** — a model may promise a slot that is no longer available.
5. **Double booking** — concurrent callers can reserve the same resource.
6. **Stale configuration** — a live conversation may read a playbook that changes halfway through the call.
7. **Unbounded collection** — an agent may collect unnecessary personal or sensitive information.
8. **Language quality gaps** — translated prompts may be grammatically correct yet operationally misleading.
9. **Silent fallback failure** — calendar or CRM outages may produce apparent success without a real booking.
10. **Outcome contamination** — model-generated classifications can become training truth without human review.

Phase 4 therefore treats intelligence as a versioned policy and workflow system, with the model used only inside bounded steps.

### Included

- versioned vertical playbooks;

- safety and escalation policies;
- model adapter boundary with deterministic fallback;
- prompt and model version tracking;

- reschedule/cancel contracts;
- human handoff and escalation queue;
- shadow-mode evaluation;

- conversation quality and booking-integrity metrics;
- tenant entitlements for vertical and booking features.

### Explicitly excluded

- broad autonomous general-purpose reception;
- diagnosis or regulated professional advice;
- binding price quotes generated by a model;
- guaranteed service availability without provider confirmation;
- arbitrary customer-authored executable prompts;
- unrestricted custom tools;
- training models on customer data without explicit governance;
- full field-service dispatch optimization;
- marketplace matching between unrelated businesses.

## Vertical playbook model

### Playbooks

Create `vertical_playbooks` with:

- organization ownership or platform ownership;
- vertical key;
- semantic version;
- lifecycle: `draft`, `review`, `shadow`, `active`, `retired`;
- supported locales;
- input and output schema versions;
- safety policy version;
- created/reviewed/activated metadata;
- immutable published content hash.

An active conversation must pin one playbook version from start to finish.

### Qualification graph

Represent qualification as a graph of deterministic nodes:

- `say`;
- `collect`;
- `classify`;
- `validate`;
- `branch`;
- `confirm`;
- `handoff`;
- `booking_search`;
- `booking_hold`;
- `booking_confirm`;
- `complete`;
- `stop`.

Each node must declare:

- stable ID;
- permitted inputs;
- expected output schema;
- maximum retries;
- timeout behavior;
- locale text;
- sensitive-data category;
- allowed next nodes;
- escalation behavior;
- whether a model may assist;
- deterministic fallback.

No model output directly chooses an arbitrary tool or route. It returns data validated against the node schema.

### Conversation runs

Create records for:

- playbook and version;
- callback job;
- current node;
- locale;
- collected structured fields;
- model invocations and versions;
- validation failures;
- escalation reason;
- booking state;
- completion outcome;
- redacted audit events.

Do not require storing raw audio or full transcripts for the workflow to operate.

## Reference vertical packs

Implement at least two contrasting packs to prove the engine is not hard-coded.

### Pack A — Appliance repair

Suggested fields:

- appliance category;
- brand if known;
- symptom;
- location;
- urgency;
- warranty or existing service relationship if approved;
- preferred visit window.

Safety boundaries:

- no electrical repair instructions;
- no promise of parts availability;
- no binding price quote;
- escalate smoke, fire, gas smell, active flooding, or immediate danger.

### Pack B — Plumbing or another approved local-service vertical

Suggested fields:

- issue category;
- active leak/flooding indicator;
- property type;
- location;
- urgency;
- shutoff status only as a factual question, not procedural advice;
- preferred visit window.

Safety boundaries:

- escalate gas, electrical contact, structural collapse, sewage exposure, or immediate danger;
- do not provide emergency instructions beyond approved fixed wording;
- do not promise arrival time until booking confirmation.

A high-harm regulated vertical must not be used merely to demonstrate flexibility.

## Safety-policy engine

Create versioned policies for:

- emergency phrases;
- prohibited claims;
- prohibited data collection;
- price/availability language;
- human-request detection;
- abusive or threatening calls;
- minors or vulnerable callers where relevant;
- unsupported language;
- repeated misunderstanding;
- call duration and question limits;
- recording/consent configuration;
- business-specific exclusions.

### Safety ordering

Safety rules execute before model interpretation where deterministic matching is possible.

Recommended order:

```text
identity and consent
  → emergency/prohibited-content check
  → language check
  → vertical eligibility
  → bounded qualification
  → booking eligibility
  → confirmation
```

### Human handoff

A handoff must create a durable record containing:

- business and organization;
- caller;
- callback job and conversation run;
- reason;
- urgency;
- collected safe context;
- requested response channel;
- status and owner;
- timestamps.

The caller must not be told a human has accepted the handoff unless the system has confirmed that state.

## Model adapter

### Interface

Provide a model abstraction supporting operations such as:

- classify an answer into an approved enum;
- extract structured fields;
- detect language;
- summarize collected context;
- select among explicitly allowed branch labels.

### Constraints

- JSON/schema output only;
- strict timeout;
- bounded token budget;
- no unrestricted tool calls;
- no hidden business secrets in prompts;
- model and prompt version logged;
- deterministic retry limit;
- fallback to a safe fixed prompt or human handoff;
- feature flag per tenant and playbook;
- test double for all automated tests.

### Prompt lifecycle

Prompts are versioned artifacts. Promotion sequence:

```text
draft → offline eval → review → shadow → limited active → active
```

Changing a prompt creates a new version rather than mutating historical behavior.

## Multilingual support

### Locale packs

Each playbook locale includes:

- business identity introduction;
- disclosure that the assistant is automated;
- every fixed question;
- retry language;
- confirmation language;
- emergency/handoff language;
- no-booking/fallback language;
- SMS/WhatsApp lead and booking templates.

### Fallback

- Detect from a small approved locale set.
- Confirm language when confidence is low.
- Fall back to the business default language.
- Offer human follow-up when unsupported.
- Never silently switch language in a way that changes consent or safety meaning.

### Evaluation

Test with native-reviewed examples, accents, code-switching, noisy input, and short colloquial answers. Synthetic translation alone does not constitute live quality proof.

## Booking domain model

### Resources

- `booking_resources` — staff, team, service bay, room, or generic capacity;
- `service_types` — duration, buffers, eligibility, location, required resource class;
- `availability_rules` — recurring schedules and exceptions;
- `booking_integrations` — provider and credential reference;
- `booking_holds` — short-lived exclusive reservations;
- `bookings` — requested, held, confirmed, canceled, failed, completed;
- `booking_events` — immutable audit trail.

### Booking flow

```text
qualification complete
  → determine service type
  → search approved availability
  → present bounded options
  → caller selects
  → atomic hold
  → provider confirmation
  → durable booking confirmation
  → notify caller and owner
```

A slot is not confirmed until both the internal booking state and external provider confirmation satisfy the adapter contract.

### Conflict safety

- Use unique constraints or locking for resource/time overlaps.
- Holds expire automatically.
- Confirmation is idempotent.
- Provider webhook retries are idempotent.
- Provider failure releases or expires the hold.
- Concurrent callers cannot receive the same capacity.
- Reschedule uses a new hold before releasing the old confirmed slot when practical.

### Provider adapters

Implement a generic interface and at least one mock/reference adapter. Optional real adapters may include Google Calendar or another calendar API, but code completion must not depend on live credentials.

Expected operations:

- list availability;
- create hold if provider supports it;
- create event/booking;
- update event;
- cancel event;
- fetch provider state;
- process provider webhook;
- map provider errors into stable internal categories.

## Booking communication

Confirmation messages must state:

- business identity;
- service type;
- date/time and timezone;
- location or service mode;
- confirmation state;
- cancellation/reschedule path;
- what is not guaranteed, such as price or exact completion time.

Do not send “confirmed” when only a request or hold exists.

## Shadow mode

Before active model or booking behavior:

- run the playbook against stored/redacted or synthetic examples;
- produce decisions without affecting callers;
- compare to deterministic/human expected outcomes;
- measure unsafe branch rate;
- measure schema failure;
- measure false escalation and missed escalation;
- measure proposed booking conflicts;
- prevent provider writes.

Shadow events must be distinguishable from live production outcomes.

- unsupported services;
- outside service area;
- emergency phrases;
- price requests;

- model malformed output;
- model timeout;
- calendar timeout;
- no availability;

- provider duplicate webhook;
- stale playbook version;
- retired playbook;
- locale fallback.

Every published playbook must pass its own golden test set.

## Tenant and entitlement integration

Phase 4 must use Phase 3 controls:

- vertical packs assigned per organization/location;
- only entitled plans may activate model or booking features;
- configuration changes require authorized roles;
- published platform playbooks cannot be edited by tenants;
- tenant overrides are validated and versioned;
- integration secrets remain server-side;
- usage ledger records model and booking operations where required;
- audit events record playbook activation and booking actions.

## Observability

Track:

- conversation start/completion;
- playbook and prompt version;
- node retries;
- model latency and schema failure;
- fallback/handoff rate;
- emergency detection;
- unsupported-service rate;
- booking search/hold/confirmation failures;
- booking conflict rate;
- caller correction rate;
- owner rejection rate;
- locale performance;
- cost per completed qualification and booking.

Do not treat model confidence as ground truth.

Phase 4 is code-complete only when all are true:

- [ ] Every conversation pins an immutable playbook version.
- [ ] Invalid or cyclic playbook graphs are rejected.
- [ ] Model output cannot invoke arbitrary tools or nodes.
- [ ] Malformed, timed-out, or unsafe model output falls back safely.
- [ ] Emergency and prohibited cases stop or hand off deterministically.
- [ ] Sensitive-data collection is bounded by node schema.
- [ ] At least two vertical packs run through the generic engine.
- [ ] Locale fallback and unsupported-language behavior are tested.
- [ ] Booking holds are conflict safe under concurrent requests.
- [ ] Duplicate provider events cannot duplicate bookings.
- [ ] A booking is never labeled confirmed before provider confirmation.
- [ ] Cancellation and reschedule are idempotent.
- [ ] Tenants cannot activate unentitled playbooks or integrations.
- [ ] Existing non-booking lead flow remains functional.
- [ ] Golden and adversarial playbook fixtures pass.
