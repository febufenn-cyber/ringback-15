# Founder Interview Script

The interview is designed to reveal current behaviour, not collect compliments about an AI idea.

## Rules for the interviewer

- Do not explain Ringback during the first two-thirds of the conversation.
- Ask for a recent example instead of a general opinion.
- Distinguish facts, estimates, and guesses in the notes.
- Ask permission before viewing call logs or customer information.
- Do not ask, “Would you use this?” Ask what they did last time.
- Do not count verbal enthusiasm as willingness to pay.

## Opening

> I am researching what happens when local service businesses cannot answer incoming calls. I am not selling anything during this conversation. I want to understand your current process using recent examples.

## Business context

1. What services generate most of your new enquiries?
2. Where do new customers usually find your phone number?
3. Who answers calls during business hours?
4. What happens after hours, while travelling, or while doing a job?
5. Which calls are most valuable or urgent?

## Recent-behaviour questions

1. Think about the last business call you missed. What happened next?
2. How long did it take before anyone returned it?
3. How did you know whether the caller was a new lead, an existing customer, or spam?
4. Did the caller answer when you called back?
5. Did that enquiry become an appointment or job?
6. What happened to the last five missed calls you remember?

## Quantification

Ask the owner to separate known facts from estimates:

- incoming calls per day/week;
- missed calls per day/week;
- percentage believed to be new enquiries;
- typical callback delay;
- percentage manually recovered;
- average booking value;
- approximate gross margin or contribution from an average job;
- busiest days and hours;
- languages callers use;
- percentage of calls from existing customers, suppliers, spam, and wrong numbers.

## Workflow mapping

1. Does the business use call forwarding, voicemail, a shared phone, or multiple SIMs?
2. Can two employees return the same missed call accidentally?
3. Does the owner text or WhatsApp before calling back?
4. What details are required before deciding whether to take a job?
5. What information should never be promised without a human?
6. What makes a lead “good,” “bad,” or “urgent”?
7. How is a lead recorded today?
8. How does the business know whether the job was won or lost?

## Trust and risk

1. Would customers recognize a callback from another number?
2. Must the callback display the existing business number or name?
3. How should an automated assistant identify itself?
4. Are calls currently recorded? How is consent handled?
5. Which conversations must always go directly to a human?
6. What customer data is too sensitive to store?
7. Have business numbers ever been marked as spam?

## Controlled concept test

Only after mapping the current workflow:

> Imagine that after a genuine missed call, the customer receives a clearly identified callback or message from your business. It asks at most four approved questions and sends you a lead card with buttons to call, accept, or reject the lead.

Then ask:

1. Where would this fail in your business?
2. Which callers should never receive it?
3. What would make you switch it off after one day?
4. What evidence would make you pay for it?
5. Would you permit a small, supervised pilot using an approved script?
6. Would you share outcomes such as booked, won, lost, and approximate job value?

## Commitment ladder

Record the highest commitment actually obtained:

0. Conversation only.
1. Introduction to another business.
2. Shares anonymized call-log counts.
3. Allows screen-share audit of recent missed calls.
4. Approves a controlled recovery experiment.
5. Agrees to provide lead outcomes.
6. Signs a paid pilot or prepays.

Do not label levels 0–2 as strong validation.

## Post-interview synthesis

Within 15 minutes, record:

- strongest observed pain;
- strongest contradiction;
- current workaround;
- estimated lost value;
- trust requirement;
- qualification questions;
- disallowed behaviour;
- commitment level;
- one reason the interview should reduce confidence in Ringback.

Use `templates/interview-notes.md` for each interview.
